About

Arif Istiaq is a postdoctoral associate at Washington University in St. Louis. His research focuses on genomic imprinting regulation in human development and disease, particularly cancer. He completed his PhD in developmental neurobiology, where he discovered regulators of RNA splicing defects affecting stem cell differentiation in hydrocephalus. He holds a BS and MS in Microbiology, with a focus on microbial genetics and metagenomics.

Research

Genomic Imprinting and Epigenetic Regulation

Investigating how imprinting is established and maintained, and how disruption contributes to developmental disorders and cancer.

Secreted Factors and Epigenetic Signaling

Exploring how extracellular signals influence chromatin states and imprinting regulation.

RNA Splicing and Stem Cell Differentiation

Identifying splicing regulators that control neural development and disease phenotypes.
