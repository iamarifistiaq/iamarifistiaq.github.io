About

Arif Istiaq is a Postdoctoral Associate at Washington University in St. Louis, where he studies genomic imprinting and epigenetic regulation in human development and disease, with a focus on cancer. He completed his PhD in developmental neurobiology, uncovering regulators of RNA splicing defects that impact stem cell differentiation in hydrocephalus. He holds a BS and MS in Microbiology, with training in microbial genetics and metagenomics.
